Professional Commercial Epoxy Flooring Installation in New Jersey

Jersey Elite Coatings specializes in heavy-duty commercial epoxy flooring systems designed to meet the demanding requirements of New Jersey's industrial and commercial facilities.

New Jersey's position as a major logistics hub, with its proximity to the Port of Newark and major highways like I-95, I-80, and the New Jersey Turnpike, makes it home to thousands of warehouses, distribution centers, and manufacturing facilities. These commercial spaces require flooring that can withstand constant forklift traffic, heavy machinery, chemical spills, and the wear and tear of 24/7 operations. Our commercial epoxy flooring systems are engineered specifically for these challenges, providing a seamless, durable surface that maintains its integrity even under the most demanding conditions.

The Northeast's climate presents unique challenges for commercial flooring. The freeze-thaw cycles common in New Jersey, combined with road salt tracked in during winter months, can destroy inferior flooring systems. Our commercial-grade epoxy formulations are specifically designed to handle these temperature fluctuations and chemical exposures. We use 100% solids epoxy systems with a minimum thickness of 1/8 inch, ensuring your floor can handle thermal expansion and contraction without cracking or delaminating.

Beyond durability, commercial epoxy flooring offers significant operational advantages for New Jersey businesses. The seamless, non-porous surface eliminates cracks and crevices where bacteria can harbor, making it ideal for food processing facilities that must meet FDA and USDA requirements. The high-gloss finish reflects light, reducing lighting costs in large warehouse spaces by up to 30%. Additionally, the chemical-resistant properties of our epoxy systems mean spills can be quickly cleaned without damaging the floor, minimizing downtime and maintenance costs.

Our Commercial Installation Process

Minimal disruption to your operations with professional project management from start to finish.

1

Site Assessment & Planning

Comprehensive evaluation of your facility including moisture testing, substrate analysis, and operational requirements. We develop a detailed project timeline that minimizes business disruption, often scheduling work during nights, weekends, or planned shutdowns.

2

Professional Surface Preparation

Diamond grinding or shot blasting to achieve proper concrete surface profile (CSP 3-4). All cracks are routed and filled, expansion joints are properly addressed, and moisture mitigation systems are applied where needed. This critical step ensures decades of performance.

3

Multi-Layer Application

Application of primer, base coat, broadcast layer (if specified), and protective topcoat. Each layer is applied at precise thickness using professional equipment. We maintain strict quality control with wet film thickness gauges and ensure proper cure between coats.

4

Quality Assurance & Documentation

Final inspection including adhesion testing, thickness verification, and slip resistance measurement. We provide complete documentation including warranty certificates, maintenance guidelines, and as-built specifications for your records.

Commercial Epoxy Flooring FAQs

How long will commercial epoxy flooring last in a warehouse environment?

With proper installation and maintenance, commercial epoxy flooring typically lasts 15-20 years in warehouse environments. Heavy-traffic areas may show wear after 10-12 years but can be recoated without complete replacement. Our 5-year commercial warranty covers any defects in materials or workmanship.

What's the cost of commercial epoxy flooring per square foot in New Jersey?

Commercial epoxy flooring in New Jersey typically ranges from $3.50 to $8.00 per square foot, depending on the system selected. Standard solid-color systems start at $3.50/sq ft, while specialized systems with chemical resistance, anti-static properties, or decorative finishes can reach $8.00/sq ft. Volume discounts apply for projects over 10,000 square feet.

Can you install epoxy flooring without shutting down our facility?

Yes, we specialize in phased installations that keep your business operational. We can work nights, weekends, or section off areas to complete the flooring in stages. Fast-cure formulations allow return to light traffic in 24 hours, with full cure in 72 hours. We'll develop a installation schedule that minimizes disruption to your operations.

How does epoxy compare to polished concrete for commercial use?

While polished concrete offers durability, epoxy provides superior chemical resistance, customization options, and repairable surfaces. Epoxy creates a protective barrier over concrete, preventing moisture penetration and chemical damage. It's also more forgiving of imperfect substrates and offers better slip resistance options for safety compliance.

What maintenance is required for commercial epoxy floors?

Commercial epoxy floors require minimal maintenance - daily dust mopping or auto-scrubbing with neutral pH cleaners. Deep cleaning quarterly with specialized equipment removes built-up residue. Unlike VCT or other flooring, there's no stripping, waxing, or resealing required. This reduces maintenance costs by up to 60% compared to traditional commercial flooring.

Is commercial epoxy flooring OSHA compliant for slip resistance?

Yes, our commercial epoxy systems can be customized to meet or exceed OSHA slip resistance requirements. We offer various aggregate broadcast options that provide texture while maintaining cleanability. Slip resistance is measured and documented using ASTM D2047 standards, with coefficients of friction typically exceeding 0.6.
