Industrial Installation Process

Minimizing operational disruption while delivering maximum performance.

1

Facility Assessment & Planning

Comprehensive facility evaluation including substrate testing, moisture analysis, and contamination assessment. We develop detailed project plans that work around your production schedule, identifying opportunities for phased installation to maintain operations.

2

Industrial Surface Preparation

Shot blasting or diamond grinding to achieve CSP 3-5 profile required for industrial applications. All cracks are routed and filled with rigid epoxy, joints are properly treated, and any contaminated concrete is removed and replaced.

3

Primer & Build Coats

Application of penetrating primer to seal concrete and ensure maximum adhesion. Multiple build coats create the required thickness (typically 1/8" to 1/4") for heavy industrial use. Special additives incorporated based on facility requirements.

4

Specialized System Application

Installation of specific system components: conductive ground planes for ESD floors, aggregate broadcasting for slip resistance, or chemical-resistant topcoats. Safety markings and designated zones integrated at this stage.

5

Quality Control & Documentation

Comprehensive testing including adhesion pull tests, thickness measurements, and electrical resistance testing for ESD systems. Complete documentation provided for regulatory compliance and warranty records.

Industrial Epoxy Flooring FAQs

How much does industrial epoxy flooring cost per square foot?

Industrial epoxy flooring typically ranges from $4 to $12 per square foot installed, depending on the system requirements. Standard industrial systems start at $4-6/sq ft, chemical-resistant systems run $6-8/sq ft, and specialized ESD or extreme chemical-resistant systems can reach $10-12/sq ft. Large facilities over 20,000 sq ft often qualify for volume discounts.

How long does industrial epoxy installation take?

Installation time depends on facility size and system complexity. A typical 10,000 sq ft installation takes 5-7 days: 2 days for surface preparation, 2-3 days for coating application, and 1-2 days for cure time before light traffic. We often work nights and weekends to minimize operational disruption. Fast-cure systems allow return to service in 24-48 hours.

Can industrial epoxy be installed without shutting down operations?

Yes, we specialize in phased installations that keep your facility operational. We can section off areas, work during off-hours, or coordinate with planned maintenance schedules. Our fast-cure systems minimize downtime, and we've completed many projects with zero production interruption.

What's the difference between industrial and commercial epoxy?

Industrial epoxy is significantly thicker (1/8" to 1/4" vs 1/16"), uses stronger resins with higher chemical resistance, and includes specialized additives for extreme conditions. Industrial systems handle heavier loads (15,000 PSI vs 10,000 PSI), resist harsher chemicals, and meet stricter regulatory requirements like FDA, USDA, and OSHA standards.

How long will industrial epoxy flooring last?

Properly installed industrial epoxy flooring lasts 20-30 years with appropriate maintenance. Factors affecting lifespan include traffic intensity, chemical exposure, thermal cycling, and maintenance practices. We've documented installations from 2005 still performing excellently. Compare this to concrete sealers (2-3 years) or epoxy paint (3-5 years).

Does industrial epoxy meet OSHA slip resistance requirements?

Yes, our industrial systems are customized to meet or exceed OSHA's coefficient of friction requirements (0.5 dry, 0.5 wet minimum). We achieve this through aggregate broadcasting, textured finishes, or specialized anti-slip additives while maintaining cleanability. Every installation includes slip resistance testing and documentation.

Can industrial epoxy handle hot equipment and thermal shock?

Our thermal-shock resistant systems handle temperatures from -40¬∞F to 350¬∞F and rapid temperature changes. This is critical for food processing areas with steam cleaning, forging operations, or transitions between freezers and production areas. Special formulations are available for extreme heat applications up to 500¬∞F.

What maintenance does industrial epoxy require?

Industrial epoxy requires minimal maintenance: daily sweeping or scrubbing with auto-scrubbers, weekly degreasing in heavy-use areas, and annual professional inspection. Unlike bare concrete that requires frequent sealing, epoxy maintains its properties without reapplication. We provide detailed maintenance protocols specific to your facility's operations.

The Critical Role of Industrial Flooring in Manufacturing Excellence

Understanding Industrial Flooring Demands

Industrial facilities in New Jersey operate under extreme conditions that would destroy conventional flooring within months. Consider a typical pharmaceutical manufacturing facility in New Brunswick: forklifts carrying 8,000-pound loads traverse the same paths hundreds of times daily, creating intense point loading and abrasion. Production areas experience chemical spills ranging from concentrated sanitizers to active pharmaceutical ingredients. Clean rooms require surfaces that won't harbor bacteria while withstanding daily VHP (vaporized hydrogen peroxide) sterilization cycles. Traditional concrete simply cannot meet these demands ‚Äì it cracks under load, absorbs chemicals, and harbors contaminants in its porous structure.

The financial implications of flooring failure in industrial settings are staggering. A food processing plant in Camden recently experienced a flooring failure that resulted in a 48-hour shutdown, costing $380,000 in lost production, $50,000 in emergency repairs, and triggering a USDA inspection that nearly resulted in additional fines. Beyond direct costs, flooring problems create safety hazards leading to worker injuries, equipment damage from concrete dust, and product contamination requiring costly recalls. Our industrial epoxy systems are engineered with failure prevention as the primary objective, providing peace of mind that your flooring will perform reliably for decades.

Advanced Industrial Epoxy Technology

Modern industrial epoxy flooring represents a convergence of polymer chemistry, engineering, and application science. Our systems utilize advanced novolac, vinyl ester, and modified epoxy resins that create cross-linked polymer networks with exceptional strength and chemical resistance. The molecular structure of these resins can be tailored to specific industrial requirements ‚Äì longer chain polymers for flexibility in thermal cycling environments, densely cross-linked structures for maximum chemical resistance, or conductive polymers for static dissipation.

The thickness of industrial epoxy systems ‚Äì typically 1/8" to 1/4" compared to 1/16" for commercial applications ‚Äì provides crucial performance advantages. This additional thickness creates a monolithic surface that distributes loads across a larger area, preventing the point loading failures common in thinner systems. Multiple layers allow incorporation of different functional elements: moisture barriers, conductive ground planes, chemical-resistant barriers, and wear surfaces. Each layer is engineered for its specific function while maintaining complete adhesion to adjacent layers.

Installation Excellence in Operating Facilities

Installing industrial flooring in active facilities requires military-precision planning and execution. Our project managers work with facility managers to develop installation schedules that maintain production while ensuring proper cure times and environmental conditions. We've perfected techniques for working in occupied facilities: negative air pressure containment systems prevent dust migration, low-odor formulations minimize disruption, and fast-cure systems reduce downtime. For a recent 100,000 square foot warehouse installation in Elizabeth, we completed the entire project without a single hour of operational shutdown by working in coordinated phases during off-peak hours.

Surface preparation in industrial settings often reveals additional challenges ‚Äì contaminated concrete from years of chemical exposure, deteriorated joints from heavy traffic, or moisture issues from damaged vapor barriers. Our preparation process addresses these comprehensively. Contaminated concrete is removed and replaced, joints are rebuilt with semi-rigid epoxy designed for movement, and moisture mitigation systems are installed where needed. This thorough preparation ensures the new flooring system performs optimally for its entire service life.
